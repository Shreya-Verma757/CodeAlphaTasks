Mary Kom is an Indian Olympic boxer from Manipur. She is the only woman to become World Amateur Boxing champion for a record six times, and the only woman boxer to have won a medal in each one of the seven world championships. She has also won a bronze medal at the 2012 London Olympics and a gold medal at the 2014 Asian Games.

Early Life

Mary Kom was born on March 1, 1983 in Kangathei, Manipur, India. She was the eldest of three children in her family. Her parents were tenant farmers who struggled to make ends meet. Mary grew up helping her parents in the fields and taking care of her younger siblings.


In her teenage years, Mary was interested in athletics and began training in running and javelin. However, she was inspired to take up boxing after watching Dingko Singh win gold at the 1998 Asian Games.

Boxing Career

Mary Kom began her boxing career in 2000 at the age of 17. Despite facing opposition from her family and community, she persisted with her training and went on to win the Manipur State Boxing Championship that same year.


In 2001, Mary won a silver medal at the Women's World Amateur Boxing Championships in Scranton, Pennsylvania, USA. She went on to win gold at the same event in 2002, and repeated the feat in 2005, 2006, 2008, 2010 and 2018.


Mary also won a bronze medal at the 2012 London Olympics, and a gold medal at the 2014 Asian Games. In addition to her boxing achievements, she has been awarded the Padma Shri, Padma Bhushan and Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna awards by the Government of India.

Retirement

In November 2017, Mary Kom announced that she would retire from boxing after the 2020 Tokyo Olympics. However, due to the COVID-19 pandemic, the Olympics were postponed to 2021. Mary has since confirmed that she will compete in the 2021 Olympics.

A Tribute to Mary Kom

Mary Kom is an inspiration to millions of people around the world. Her perseverance, determination and hard work have made her one of the greatest boxers of all time. She has broken barriers and shattered stereotypes, proving that women can excel in any field they choose.


As we celebrate her achievements and legacy, let us also remember the struggles she faced and the sacrifices she made to reach the top. Mary Kom is not just a champion in the ring, but also a champion of the human spirit.
